So, here’s the deal—I stumbled upon one of George Hotz’s streams where he casually dove into this thing called **SLAM**. It hooked me instantly. SLAM, or **Simultaneous Localization and Mapping**, is how autonomous systems—like self-driving cars—figure out where they are (localization) while building a map of their surroundings (mapping). Naturally, I had to learn more.

Self-driving cars rely on sensors like **LiDAR**, **cameras**, **radar**, and **IMUs** to collect environmental data. SLAM combines all this into a live, constantly updating map, even when GPS isn’t reliable. It’s what allows these systems to “see” and adapt in real-time.

The tech side is fascinating: algorithms like **Extended Kalman Filters** and **Particle Filters** predict and refine the vehicle’s position. Meanwhile, **sensor fusion** combines data from multiple sensors into something coherent. SLAM doesn’t just handle static environments either; it deals with dynamic changes like pedestrians and moving vehicles.

Watching SLAM-powered robots and cars navigate in real-time is mind-blowing—it’s like they’re “thinking” their way through the world. I’m still scratching the surface, but exploring SLAM has been an incredible rabbit hole. If you’re into robotics or self-driving cars, it’s worth diving into.
